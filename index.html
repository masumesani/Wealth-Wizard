What is the 50/30/20 rule?

The 50/30/20 rule is a budgeting method that allocates your net income into three categories:

  • 50% for Needs: Essential expenses like housing, food, utilities, and transportation.
  • 30% for Fun Fund: Discretionary spending like entertainment, dining out, and hobbies.
  • 20% for Savings: Financial goals such as investments, emergency funds, or debt repayment.

This simple framework helps you prioritize essential expenses while setting aside funds for both enjoyment and future financial security.
